# 1080 to Zero - risk register and review checklist

Status: launch gate  
Last updated: 2026-07-11

## Scientific risk

Risk: Overstating replacement readiness.

Control:

- Say lower-impact replacements exist for several major uses, not every use.
- Name rabbit control, remote programs and aerial baiting as transition gaps.
- Have the evidence dossier reviewed by an animal-welfare scientist or veterinarian before launch.

Risk: Treating all alternatives as humane.

Control:

- Use "lower-impact" rather than "humane" unless a source supports the specific claim.
- Assess non-target risk and welfare effects for PAPP, sodium nitrite, shooting, trapping and non-lethal methods.

Risk: Ignoring conservation and livestock-protection objectives.

Control:

- Use "not no control, better control" framing.
- Include conservation, farming and rural transition voices where possible.

## Legal and regulatory risk

Risk: Claiming the AAWS can directly ban 1080.

Control:

- State that AAWS can set national direction and commitments, but product approvals and use restrictions require APVMA and state/territory implementation.

Risk: Hard-coding minister names that change.

Control:

- Store targets by role.
- Re-verify office-holders before every action launch and submission send.

Risk: Publishing unsafe bait location data.

Control:

- Use delayed or aggregated public data.
- Redact exact active bait locations.

## Privacy and data risk

Risk: GitHub Pages collecting supporter data.

Control:

- Keep all supporter data inside Action Network or another reviewed provider.
- Do not add API keys, target CSVs or private exports to the public repo.

Risk: Consent confusion.

Control:

- Separate campaign consent from marketing consent.
- No pre-ticked marketing boxes.
- Confirm unsubscribe, suppression, export and deletion workflows before public launch.

## Reputation risk

Risk: Campaign dismissed as anti-farmer or anti-conservation.

Control:

- Lead with funded transition and effective management.
- Use case studies showing practical alternatives and transition needs.

Risk: Campaign copy becomes too broad.

Control:

- Keep every public message tied to AAWS 2027, 1080 phase-out, 2030 endpoint, replacement funding and reporting.

## Launch checklist

Public page:

- Campaign page added and source-backed.
- No graphic imagery.
- Correction route available.
- Primary action marked testing until provider checks pass.

Evidence:

- Welfare claims checked against RSPCA and government sources.
- Alternatives claims checked against Agriculture Victoria and PestSmart.
- Limitations section included.
- Legal pathway checked.

Targets:

- Federal MP matching tested.
- State MP matching tested.
- Minister target lists verified.
- AWTG submission list verified.
- Internal delivery test complete.

Privacy:

- Consent notice reviewed.
- No supporter data in repo.
- Export and deletion process documented.
- Suppression and unsubscribe tested.

Comms:

- Default letter reviewed.
- Media lines reviewed.
- Expert open letter permissions set.
- FOI templates reviewed for safety and privacy.

Go/no-go:

- Legal review complete.
- Scientific review complete.
- Action Network delivery logs clean.
- Manual correction contact active.
- Decision-maker tracker ready.
