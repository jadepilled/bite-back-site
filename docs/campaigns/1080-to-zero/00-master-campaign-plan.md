# 1080 to Zero - campaign master document

Status: working campaign plan  
Last updated: 2026-07-11  
Public campaign page: `/campaigns/1080-to-zero/`

## Campaign proposition

Australia is renewing the Australian Animal Welfare Strategy. The campaign asks governments to use that process to set a national endpoint for sodium fluoroacetate, or 1080, and to fund replacement pathways that protect native animals, ecosystems, livestock and rural communities without permanent reliance on severe poisoning.

The campaign does not argue for abandoning invasive-animal management. It argues for better control, public reporting and a fixed transition deadline.

## Core demand

Include this commitment in the renewed AAWS and the Animals in the wild chapter:

> Australian governments will eliminate the use of sodium fluoroacetate, or 1080, for vertebrate animal control no later than 31 December 2030. From adoption of the Strategy, jurisdictions will require demonstrably lower-welfare-impact methods wherever they can effectively achieve the stated management objective, while funding species-specific replacement pathways for remaining uses.

## Public ask

Ask federal, state and territory decision-makers to support:

1. A national 1080 phase-out in the renewed AAWS.
2. Immediate replacement where lower-impact methods are already suitable.
3. A funded transition for remote, aerial and other unresolved uses.
4. Annual public reporting of 1080 quantities, target species, locations, outcomes and non-target incidents.

## Strategic opportunity

DAFF describes the AAWS process as a renewal, with five proposed chapters. The Animals in the wild chapter covers wildlife, introduced and feral animals, and interactions between humans and animals in the wild. DAFF's roadmap shows chapter development and engagement continuing through 2026, with ministerial and intergovernmental processes leading toward finalisation.

The AAWS will not itself amend every state poison rule or APVMA product registration. Its value is that it can set a national policy commitment, timetable, implementation plan and accountability framework through the Animal Welfare Task Group, senior officials and agriculture ministers.

## Theory of change

The campaign works if Bite Back can make a 1080 endpoint feel like the responsible middle position: not "no control", not indefinite poison, but planned replacement.

The campaign must therefore do four things at once:

- Make the welfare problem visible without graphic shock material.
- Acknowledge legitimate conservation, biosecurity and livestock-protection objectives.
- Show that lower-impact alternatives already exist for important uses.
- Put pressure on governments to fund and timetable the harder replacement work.

## Decision path

Primary pathway:

1. Influence the Animals in the wild chapter and overarching AAWS text before finalisation.
2. Secure a commitment from agriculture ministers to a national 1080 phase-out and annual reporting.
3. Move implementation into APVMA approvals, state and territory chemical controls, agency operating procedures and procurement.
4. Track whether jurisdictions actually reduce and then end routine 1080 use by 31 December 2030.

## Campaign phases

### Phase 1 - Evidence and coalition

Publish or prepare:

- Evidence dossier.
- Proposed AAWS wording.
- Expert open letter.
- Minister and AWTG briefing pack.
- Non-graphic visual explainer.
- FOI request templates for 1080 procurement, deployment and non-target incidents.

Success indicators:

- Expert signatories across welfare, veterinary, ecology and land-management fields.
- At least one credible rural, conservation or landholder voice willing to support funded transition language.
- Reviewed evidence pack ready before the next public AAWS consultation moment.

### Phase 2 - Constituent pressure

Launch postcode-based MP action only after Action Network testing passes.

The supporter should contact no more than four recipients:

- Federal MP.
- State or territory MP.
- State or territory agriculture or animal welfare minister.
- Commonwealth Agriculture Minister.

The supporter should be prompted to add a personal sentence. The default letter should be concise, editable and separately addressed.

Success indicators:

- Letters by electorate and jurisdiction.
- Ministerial responses recorded and quoted where appropriate.
- Public tracker updated weekly during consultation periods.

### Phase 3 - Accountability

Publish:

- Jurisdiction scorecard.
- Minister position tracker.
- AAWS text comparison.
- Public reporting gaps.
- 2030 implementation tracker if the AAWS commitment is won.

Success indicators:

- AAWS draft or final text includes phase-out, replacement funding, public reporting or independent welfare review language.
- At least one jurisdiction announces replacement or transparency measures.
- APVMA or state regulators acknowledge implementation pathway.

## Success metrics

Minimum campaign win:

- AAWS includes national language requiring replacement of 1080 wherever lower-impact methods can achieve the management objective.

Major campaign win:

- AAWS includes a national phase-out commitment, annual reporting and funded replacement program.

Full campaign win:

- Australian governments commit to ending all routine 1080 use by 31 December 2030, with APVMA approvals, state permits and agency procedures amended before that date.

## Message architecture

Primary line:

> Australia is writing its animal welfare future. Write out 1080.

Support line:

> Effective control should not depend indefinitely on severe suffering.

Short campaign lines:

- 1080 to Zero.
- Protect wildlife. Protect livestock. Replace 1080.
- A welfare strategy needs a deadline.
- No permanent poison. A permanent transition.

## Scientific integrity guardrails

Do not claim:

- 1080 is banned everywhere else.
- 1080 always kills indiscriminately.
- Every current use has a simple replacement now.
- PAPP or sodium nitrite has no welfare or non-target risk.
- A single alternative can replace every use everywhere.
- Ending 1080 has no cost, operational or conservation implications.

Use this framing instead:

> Lower-impact replacements already exist for several major uses. Where gaps remain, Australia needs a funded transition deadline rather than indefinite reliance on 1080.
