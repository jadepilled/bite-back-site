# 1080 to Zero - media, coalition and FOI plan

Status: working plan  
Last updated: 2026-07-11

## Media hooks

Strongest hooks:

- Expert open letter calling for a 2030 phase-out.
- FOI-based map of government 1080 procurement and deployment by jurisdiction.
- Register of reported non-target and companion-animal incidents.
- Jurisdiction scorecard comparing transparency, replacement planning and phase-out support.
- Minister position tracker.
- Case studies of landholders or agencies using lower-impact methods.

Avoid graphic suffering footage. Use documents, maps, numbers, ministerial responses and clean visual lock-ups.

## Coalition strategy

Invite input before asking for endorsement. Do not presume support from farmers, conservation groups or First Nations organisations.

Potential coalition categories:

- Veterinarians and animal-welfare scientists.
- Wildlife carers.
- Companion-dog and working-dog organisations.
- Dingo researchers and advocates.
- Conservation scientists who support humane transition.
- Landholders using integrated management or alternatives.
- First Nations organisations interested in animal and Country management.
- Rural mental-health or farming voices who can speak to transition funding.

## Coalition pitch

The campaign is not asking governments to walk away from invasive-animal management. It asks them to set an endpoint for 1080, replace it where lower-impact methods already work, and fund the transition for the difficult cases.

## FOI priorities

Request from each relevant department or agency:

- Annual 1080 procurement quantities and costs.
- Annual deployment by target species and broad region.
- Aerial baiting program records.
- Program objective and outcome monitoring documents.
- Non-target incident reports.
- Companion-animal incident reports.
- Internal assessments of PAPP, sodium nitrite and other alternatives.
- Communications with APVMA or other jurisdictions about 1080 phase-out or replacement.

## FOI safety and ethics

Do not request or publish exact active bait locations. Ask for delayed, aggregated or redacted location data where necessary to avoid public safety risks.

Do not publish personal details of incident reporters, landholders, agency field officers or private veterinary clients.

## Jurisdiction scorecard

Score each jurisdiction on:

- Public 1080 use data.
- Public non-target incident data.
- Use of lower-impact alternatives.
- Published necessity assessment before baiting.
- Annual outcome measurement.
- Phase-out support.
- Ministerial response quality.
- Transition funding.

Suggested labels:

- Leading.
- Moving.
- Opaque.
- Blocking.
- No clear position.

## Response tracker publication rules

Publish only:

- Official correspondence.
- Public statements.
- Direct quotes with source.
- Summaries that have been checked against the original text.

Do not publish:

- Private phone notes without confirmation.
- Staff names unless publicly listed or necessary.
- Supporter personal information.
