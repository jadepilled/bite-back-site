## Default supporter letter

Subject: Put a national phase-out of 1080 in the renewed Australian Animal Welfare Strategy

Dear [Title] [Surname],

I am writing as a resident of [suburb/electorate] to ask you to support a national commitment to phase out and permanently prohibit the use of sodium fluoroacetate, or 1080, in the renewed Australian Animal Welfare Strategy.

The AAWS renewal is expected to guide Australia's animal welfare direction for years to come. Its Animals in the wild chapter covers introduced and feral animals as well as wildlife. This gives governments a rare chance to set a coordinated national path away from 1080 while continuing effective protection of native animals, ecosystems, livestock and rural livelihoods.

I am not asking governments to abandon pest-animal control. I am asking governments to replace 1080 where lower-impact methods can achieve the objective, and to fund the transition for harder cases instead of leaving 1080 as a permanent default.

Available welfare evidence raises serious concerns about 1080 poisoning. RSPCA Australia does not support describing 1080 as humane. Government evidence also shows that alternatives are already available for important uses: Agriculture Victoria's review found lower relative welfare impacts for PAPP in foxes and wild dogs, sodium nitrite in feral pigs, and properly performed shooting where feasible.

Please support the following commitments in the renewed AAWS:

1. A national phase-out of 1080 ending no later than 31 December 2030.
2. Immediate replacement of 1080 where lower-impact methods are suitable and effective.
3. A funded transition program for remote, aerial and other unresolved uses.
4. Annual public reporting of 1080 quantities, target species, program objectives, measured outcomes and non-target incidents.
5. Independent welfare and efficacy assessment for every remaining use during the transition.

Please let me know whether you support placing a 1080 phase-out and final prohibition date in the renewed AAWS, and whether you will raise this with the relevant agriculture minister or through the Agriculture Ministers' Meeting.

Yours sincerely,

[Name]  
[Suburb and postcode]  
[Email]

## Media release skeleton

Headline: Bite Back calls for national 1080 phase-out in renewed animal welfare strategy

Lead:

Bite Back is calling on Australian governments to include a national commitment to phase out sodium fluoroacetate, or 1080, in the renewed Australian Animal Welfare Strategy, with routine use ending by 31 December 2030.

Quote:

Australia does not have to choose between effective animal management and permanent reliance on severe poisoning. The responsible path is to replace 1080 now where better methods are suitable, fund the hard transition work, and publish what is being used and what it is doing.

Proof points:

- The renewed AAWS includes an Animals in the wild chapter.
- APVMA regulates 1080 products up to retail supply, while states and territories control use.
- RSPCA Australia does not support describing 1080 as humane.
- Agriculture Victoria's review identifies lower relative welfare impacts for alternatives in several applications.

Call to action:

Ask your representatives to support 1080 to Zero.
